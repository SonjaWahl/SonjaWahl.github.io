Supervision and Mentorship

Science progress is a community effort. Research does not (only) happen behind closed doors in small offices but is best done through collaborations embedded in a supportive community.
Collaboration, supervision, and mentorship lie at the heart of the success of research projects, individual studies, and the scientists themselves.

I have had many influential, inspiring and guiding mentors so far who have greatly helped me in my work and beyond. With each mentor, I have experienced a different mentorship style that has helped shape the idea of the mentor I aspire to become.
Here, I list the mentees and experiences that I was fortunate enough to learn from on my way to becoming a good mentor and teacher myself:
