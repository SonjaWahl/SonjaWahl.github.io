About me

I am an Environmental Scientist who is fascinated by the complex climate system and especially intrigued by the polar areas where the fast evolving climate change is so tangible that it is both an exciting studying area and a an alert to act.

My research is centered around the interaction between the fast changing atmosphere and the integrating snowpack below. I am fascinated by snow in all its varieties and study the processes affecting it such as snow metamorphism, surface energy fluxes and wind drift. Snow is archiving climatic information and I am particularly interested in how the climate signal is formed in the snow. This signal can be preserved for thousands of years and can be retrieved in the form of ice cores. My work of studying present cryospheric processes will help to improve climate reconstruction efforts from such ice core climate proxy records.

Since December 2023, I have been the PI of the SnowDOGS project in which I am implementing isotopes in the SNOWPACK model.
