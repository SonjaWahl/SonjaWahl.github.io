Research

I am broadly interested in the climate of the polar and high alpine regions, with a focus on snow and ice. In my research, I am using stable water isotopes to better understand snow processes and snow-atmosphere interactions. I find myself at the interface of ice core and snow sciences and strive to bridge the two research communities. I have mostly worked with observational data from in-situ field campaigns or cold-laboratory experiments. Progressively my work is also including numerical models of increasing complexity.
I am interested in the following snow processes:

Blowing snow

Blowing snow

We investigated the evolution of snow physical properties and its isotopic composition during blowing snow transport. With help of the stable water isotope signal we could identify airborne snow metamorphism as a process that influnces the snow particle size and shape and their isotopic composition.

Related publications: Wahl et al. (2024), Walter et al. (2024)



Snow air exchange through sublimation and deposition

By combining fast eddy-covariance flux measurements with direct stable water isotope measurements in the vapor using cavity ring-down spectroscopy we could measure the surface flux of isotopes. Our results showed that net sublimation involves kinetic fractioantion which contradicted the conventional picture of isotope dynamics during snow sublimation.

Related publications: Wahl et al. (2021), Wahl et al. (2022)

Snow metamorphism

Snow metamorphism is the recrystallization of snow crystals in isothermal or temperature-gradient conditions. This snow process influences the snow's physical properties and leaves an isotopic fingerprint in the snow's isotopic composition.

See for example: Harris Stuart et al. (2023)



Using models to simulate snow processes

I am currently developing an isotope-enabled model of the snowpack to simulate the effect of different snow processes on the snow isotopic composition. The aim is to quantify the effect of post-depositional processes for the climate signal found in snow and ice cores.

See for example: Jafari al. (2020), Touzeau et al. (2018)

Ice core reserach

One motivation for my work is the reconstruction of climate from ice core records. Ice cores are valuable archives of climate signals and my works aims at improving the paleoclimate reconstructions.

Related work: Wahl MSc Thesis (2018)
